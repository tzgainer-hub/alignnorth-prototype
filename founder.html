In His Own Words

Why Align North exists

For more than twenty years, I've worked with bodies under stress — world-champion boxers, professional golfers, and the people who walk into Tanner Chiropractic with back, neck, hip, or joint pain that's stopped them from doing what they love.

The work spans a wide range, but the lesson has been the same one, over and over: the adjustment, the rehab, the manual therapy — all of it works. And then a few months later, the same patient is back with the same problem. The reason is simple. We treated the symptom. But the body that produced the symptom — its posture, its strength, its movement patterns — went home and kept doing what it had always done.

I saw it in elite athletes. I saw it more clearly in everyday patients. The body needs more than a session at the table; it needs to be retrained, slowly and precisely, in the environment of its actual life.

What I needed was a place I could send patients where that retraining would actually happen. Not a gym. Not a yoga studio. A private practice where every session was built around what each person's body needed, with instructors who understood movement at a clinical level.

I couldn't find one I trusted. So I built one.

Align North Scottsdale is what I wish had existed when I started practicing. Private and duet sessions only. Six specialists who think about posture, mobility, and movement the way I think about the spine. A Doctor of Physical Therapy on staff. Pilates instructors trained at the highest levels of their disciplines. Two suites at 33747 N. Scottsdale Rd, side by side, working as one team.

I send my chiropractic patients here. The members of the Golfer's Edge program I built for Desert Mountain do their off-course conditioning here. I train here myself. And if you're reading this because something hurts — or because you can feel your body not moving the way it used to — this is the place I'd send you, too.
